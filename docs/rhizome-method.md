# Rhizome: A Research Method

A record of the system we worked out together, so the thread does not get lost. Rhizome is a way of doing research, not just a place to store it. Its purpose is to mature ideas and to harvest the governing principles that connect work across disciplines.

---

## The Engine (the core, in two paragraphs)

Rhizome is a maturation engine, not a database. Knowledge does not sit in it; it ripens through it. Every idea enters as loose speculation and climbs through a set of epistemic layers (brainstorm, hypothesis, frontier, principle), and each step upward is a gate that has to be paid for. The currency is two things at once: evidence, meaning the idea has survived contact with reality, and structure, meaning the idea has exposed its own wiring as explicit relations rather than as prose. An idea cannot rise simply by being written more clearly. It rises by becoming testable, then by being tested, then by revealing the mechanism underneath it in a form precise enough to be compared against other mechanisms.

Once a thread fully matures, its principle is held in three coupled layers. Plain English states what the principle is. The physical layer describes the relational mechanism of the phenomenon. Mathematics, where it exists, is the proof-grade form, the actual language of the system. Connection across disciplines is then evaluated by matching matured threads against one another at the structural level: two principles connect when enough of their roots correspond, where a root is a causal or governing relation and not a shared noun. The match is graded by the layer at which it holds. English agreement only suggests and is mostly noise. Structural agreement is strong. Matching the form of the nondimensionalized governing equations is proof. The system grows because every confirmed connection condenses a shared core into a new principle node one layer up, and that node is itself a rhizome with roots, so it becomes matchable in turn. The abstraction ladder builds itself out of grounded threads rather than out of an AI guessing edges.

## The Three Pillars You Wanted Kept

**Layers of ideas.** The layers are not storage tiers, they are epistemic states: how much contact with reality an idea has survived. Brainstorm holds raw speculation. Hypothesis holds a claim that could be proven wrong. Frontier holds a claim with an experiment designed for it. Principle holds a claim that survived the experiment and generalized. The important objects are the transitions between layers, not the layers themselves.

**The maturity cycle.** Promotion is never free. The gate from brainstorm to hypothesis demands a falsifiable claim. The gate from hypothesis to frontier demands a designed experiment. The gate from frontier to principle demands a surviving result that generalizes. Each gate costs both evidence and explicit structure. This cycle is also what lets a personal model speculate freely and safely: anything it generates lands in the brainstorm layer and cannot climb without paying the grounding tax, so the local tool never drifts the whole system into self confirmation.

**Connecting matured ideas across disciplines.** A principle is not stored as an object. It is the invariant shared by two matured rhizomes when you align them and see which roots correspond. Same wiring, different nouns. The thermosyphon and the BWRX-300 isolation condenser match not because a label says natural circulation but because the relational skeleton lines up: a temperature difference drives a phase change, which drives a density difference, which drives buoyant flow, which yields passive transport with no pump. The matchable form, where the math exists, is the nondimensionalized governing equation, because nondimensionalization strips the units and constants and leaves the pure skeleton.

## The Asymmetry (the key correction)

The system does not need to be closed, complete, or correct about everything. It is a spark generator, not a solver. Its job is to evaluate connections that make a plausible solution visible. The robot hand example shows why this is enough: the hand already has the dexterity, so the system does not have to derive it. Once the connection is made, the work is to go and find the physics behind it and bring that physics back into your own research. The connection points; the human and the experiment follow.

This is also what resolves the biology problem. A field does not need clean governing equations for a match to be valuable, because the match only has to point somewhere worth mining. So a thread that matches at the physical and structural layer but has no mathematical form yet is not a failed match. It is the highest-value output the system can produce: a discovered frontier, a place where a strong principle exists with no governing equation behind it. The blind spot, named correctly, is the compass to unsolved interdisciplinary work, which is exactly the biology-to-engineering gap this whole idea started from.

## Main Ideas and Findings (the full brainstorm, condensed)

1. The real object is a graph, a rhizome, not a tree. Trees are strictly hierarchical and the robot hand has many parents at once. The value lives in the edges between domains, so cross-domain connections are first-class objects.
2. The target is governing principles, not surface analogies. Surface analogies are cheap and usually wrong. Extract the principle and discard the domain accidents (we did not copy flapping wings, we extracted lift).
3. Two failure modes decide everything. Signal-to-noise: generating connections is easy, ranking and pruning them is the hard part, and restraint is the dexterity. Model collapse: a closed loop that retrains on its own output drifts into confident hallucination unless every generated edge stays provisional until grounded.
4. What dies with people is not the paper. The paper is the one thing that survives. What dies is the tacit layer below the frontier: the dead ends, the rejected hunches, the reasons something felt wrong. The lower layers give that material a home and a forkable status, so an abandoned thread can be picked up years later.
5. A root must be a relation, not a noun. Counting shared nouns sends you back to surface similarity. Weighting causal and governing relations, and rewarding depth and specificity over count, is what makes the match real. Generic skeletons that fit half of science must be suppressed.
6. Maturation must produce explicit structure, not just better prose. If threads stay as text there is nothing to align and you collapse back to fuzzy embedding matches. The price of promotion includes exposing the wiring.

## People and Systems That Think This Way

This is the lineage. The novelty is not the philosophy. It is making this run as software.

- **Charles Sanders Peirce.** Abduction, deduction, induction. Generate the hunch, derive what must follow, test it. This is the continuous-growth cycle, and it maps onto your three layers: English is the abduction, math is the deduction, experiment is the induction.
- **Karl Popper.** Falsifiability. The gate from speculation to hypothesis is the demand for a claim that could be proven wrong.
- **Imre Lakatos.** A hard core of principles protected by a belt of auxiliary hypotheses under test, which is your layered structure directly. His distinction between a progressive programme that generates new predictions and a degenerating one that only patches itself is a health signal you could track per thread.
- **Dedre Gentner.** Structure-Mapping Theory, the dominant cognitive account of analogy. Analogy is shared relations, not shared attributes, and good analogies preserve higher-order relations. Her systematicity principle is your "enough roots." This is the formal backing for the entire matching idea.
- **Steven Brunton and collaborators.** SINDy, sparse identification of nonlinear dynamics, which discovers governing equations from data.
- **Silviu-Marian Udrescu and Max Tegmark.** AI Feynman, which recovers symbolic physical laws and leans on dimensional analysis to do it.
- **Lie symmetry analysis.** The deepest formal notion of two systems being the same equation: they share a symmetry group. The rigorous version of structural matching.
- **Cautionary case, Meta's Galactica.** A model that wrote confident, fabricated science and was pulled within days. The reason grounding and the gates are not optional.

## The Open Frontier (and a note on why this is real)

Automatically matching equation form and relational structure across domains is itself partly unsolved. None of the tools above do cross-domain principle matching out of the box. So the hardest problem inside Rhizome is the same kind of problem Rhizome exists to host. That is fitting, and it is where the defensible core sits: not the note graph that everyone has, but the maturation method and the principle-matching loop underneath it.

A note worth keeping. This conversation was itself an instance of the method. The thermosyphon-to-BWRX-300 connection and the move from your gut feeling about roots to Gentner's theory were the engine running, with the matching done in dialogue. That is the evidence it is useful. The method already works when a mind does it by hand. The project is to formalize what that mind was doing.
